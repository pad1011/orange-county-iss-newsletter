ISS News

What's New & Improved

Using AI to Support Smarter County Services

Orange County is finding new ways to use artificial intelligence (AI) to improve daily operations and make services more convenient for residents and employees. Across multiple departments, AI tools are helping staff manage routine tasks related to procurement, finance, information technology, document review, compliance, cybersecurity, software development, and employee support.

These tools help save time, reduce repetitive work, improve accuracy, and increase efficiency, allowing employees to focus more on serving the community. Residents are already seeing benefits through faster response times and easier access to County services and information.

County leaders say AI is being introduced carefully to support employees while following County policies and maintaining appropriate technology safeguards.

Helping Staff Find Answers Faster with Custom GPT

Orange County is using a Custom GPT tool to help 311 staff quickly find accurate information across County services and better assist residents with questions and resource navigation.

The tool reduces time spent searching for information by providing faster, more consistent responses during daily interactions and high call volumes. Staff can quickly access service details, program information, and County resources, helping improve response efficiency and reduce manual lookup efforts.

Early feedback shows the tool is improving response speed and consistency while supporting staff in delivering accurate information to residents. The effort is part of the County's broader approach to using AI tools responsibly to improve service quality and support employees in their daily work.

AI at Work

Exploring Smarter Ways to Work

AI Helping Teams Work Smarter and Faster

Orange County's Procurement Division is using artificial intelligence (AI) tools to help streamline daily operations and support employees managing high workloads. AI assists with invoice processing, contract reviews, workload assignments, change orders, and Statements of Work (SOWs), while also improving compliance and document quality.

The technology is already being used as part of the County's broader AI initiative. Current estimates show these tools save approximately 7,954 work hours and nearly $200,000 annually while improving consistency, reducing delays, and helping teams manage growing workloads more efficiently.

Quick Tip

Protect Your Devices with County Antivirus Software

Orange County employees can use Sophos antivirus software for approved home use with their @ocfl.net email account. Sophos helps protect devices from malware, ransomware, and phishing attacks while supporting secure remote work.

  • Installation instructions and additional resources are available through the internal ISS portal using County login credentials.
  • Contractors and interns may have different access permissions and should contact ISS for assistance.

Important: Using approved antivirus software on home devices helps protect County systems and reduces cybersecurity risks.

Cybersecurity Corner

Stay Safe & Informed

Secure Remote Access Starts with Safe Connections

Orange County continues improving remote access tools to help employees work securely and efficiently from different locations. Updated virtual private network (VPN) technology strengthens security, improves connection reliability, and supports a smoother experience for staff accessing County systems remotely.

Ongoing improvements focus on usability, reliability, and cost efficiency while helping protect County networks and information during remote work activities.

Before Purchasing Software, Start with ISS

All software requests must go through ISS for review and approval before any purchase is made. Employees should work with their ISS project lead or submit a request through the formal intake process to help ensure security, compliance, and compatibility requirements are met.

Following the process helps reduce security risks, avoid duplicate tools and unnecessary spending, and support County AI and IT governance standards while helping departments make informed technology decisions.
